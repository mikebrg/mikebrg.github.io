Santa Luċija

Saint Lucia
Santa Luċija is a village in the Southern Region of Malta, with a population of 2,617 as of 2021. It is one of the modern towns developed in Malta during the 20th century. By virtue of an article which appeared in Government Gazette of 7 July 1961 the area between Tal-Barrani (Tarxien By-Pass) and Luqa By-Pass. Santa Luċija was named after a 16th-century chapel located in the vicinity. The Parish Church is dedicated to Pope Pius X. It is home of the Chinese Garden of Serenity (a Chinese-built public garden). Located there is a hypogeum which was discovered in 1973.
Established: 7th July 1961
Region: Southern Region
Electoral District: 4
Area: 0.7 Sq Km
Population: 2,617 (2021)
Postal Code: SLC
Patron Saint: Pope Pius X
Feast Date: 3rd Sunday of August

Attractions and places of interest

Chinese Garden of Serenity, a Chinese traditional garden
Sphere of Life, a commemorative monument by Paul Vella Critien
Votive Column, a monument representing fertility

Santa Luċija is twinned with:

Carlentini, Italy
Gabiano, Italy
Gusu District, China
