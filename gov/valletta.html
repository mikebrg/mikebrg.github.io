Residents of Valletta

Back in 2012, a small group was created to help residents whenever possible voluntarily. As of the year 2021 Residenti Beltin has been registered to officially work in the interest of residents.

The Party itself urges every resident to join it in calling for the outpouring and privatisation to stop, and protect it with facts. Together with the purpose of strengthening the rights of all residents.

Objectives & Principles

The party which is in favour of humbly working towards the Valletta residents, for the unanimous interest of each resident without looking at the colour of the individuals thus the party falls under an Independent Party. Unfortunately many are elected and then abuse the trust given to them. By introducing collective proposals in the field of Local Council Election, they guarantee a new reform thus preventing the party from doing what it wants if it rises to the Council.

The party is committed to providing back the dignity, relief, respect and rights that every resident should have. According to the law that protects the individual inside his home and in public. It operates as both a 'Neighbourhood Watch' and a 'Pressure Group'.

The party is committed to striking a balance between residents and the commercial sector. This comes after the party was left with the ongoing grievances of the residents, who fell silent and targeted by many towards them.

The party strongly believes in the environmental, traditional, cultural and heritage needs and protection of national heritage located in Valletta. For the sake of quality, a better life and with full respect for the rights of future generations in Valletta. In addition, the party is committed to strengthening and honouring the title by UNESCO World Heritage, together with any obligation that this prestige was officially granted in 1980 over Valletta.

The party urges to be listened to, and to convey the voice of the residents with absolute transparency. To proceed with no intentions or decisions that go against residents especially those in vulnerable categories. Assist residents as an intermediary person where and when possible. With a view to giving the Council back to the residents, the passing of the party, and where it believes that it will be an example in every other locality.
